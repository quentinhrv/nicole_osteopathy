ABOUT ME

My name is Nicole, and I've just arrived in Vancouver, where a new adventure awaits me as an osteopathic practitioner. My chose to become an osteopathic practitioner, it stems from my desire to work in the healthcare field while favoring natural approaches. Osteopathy has allowed me to delve into the intricacies of the human body, offering the opportunity to heal holistically, with my own hands.

I Graduated with a 5 years full-time diploma (4800 hours of education) in Osteopathic Manual Therapy from the Osteopathic College of Bordeaux in France. My comprehensive 5-year training has allowed me to acquire a diverse expertise and explore various treatment methods. During my studies, I participated in internships in sports competitions such as crossfit, swimming, and races, as well as with disabled children. These experiences have shaped my understanding of osteopathy, enabling me to adapt my practice to the specific needs of my patients.

As a member of Osteopathy BC, I strive to stay informed about the latest advances in the field and maintain high professional standards. My therapeutic approach includes a range of techniques, from myofascial manipulation to visceral mobilization and myotensive techniques, all tailored to the individual needs of my patients.
